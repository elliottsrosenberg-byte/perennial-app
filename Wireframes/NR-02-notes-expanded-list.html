Walnut slab idea for gallery show

Created Apr 16 · 2:34 PM
Modified Today · 2h ago

Need to source 8/4 walnut stock for the gallery proposal. The client specifically requested live-edge slabs, roughly 36" × 72" dimension range. Looking at suppliers in Michigan and Indiana for better pricing on thicker material.

Initial concept: three sculptural table bases in complementary wood tones—walnut, ash, and cherry. Each roughly 18" H, graduating in size. The slab surfaces would be sealed with matte polyurethane to highlight grain. Estimated assembly time per piece is about 12–14 hours with finishing.

Budget considerations: Raw material roughly $400–600 per piece, finishing supplies ~$150 total, hardware $80/piece. Labor at current rate would be $1,800–2,100 per item. This puts the finished cost around $2,400–2,800 each if sold at 40% markup.

Next steps: Email three suppliers for quotes on live-edge walnut. Confirm delivery timeline with the gallery. Review the booth layout in Figma to finalize positioning and scale.
