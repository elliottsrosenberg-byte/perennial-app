# The press landscape

category: press

## Key outlets

Design and interiors press that matters for independent designers includes: Wallpaper*, Dezeen, Sight Unseen, Architectural Digest, Elle Decor, Dwell, Galerie, Surface, T Magazine (The New York Times), and Coveteur.

## What press does (and doesn't do)

Press rarely translates directly to sales. What it builds is credibility — with galleries, collectors, and trade clients who take you more seriously once you've been covered. Treat press as reputation infrastructure, not a sales channel.

## How to pitch

Editors receive hundreds of pitches weekly. Short, clear, well-photographed pitches win. Lead with the news or the hook, attach high-quality images, and make the editor's job easy. Time press releases around fair openings or new-work launches, when editors are already looking for design stories.

## Timing

Coordinate press outreach with the fair calendar and product launches. A pitch tied to a moment (a Salone debut, a new edition, a gallery show) is far more likely to land than an unanchored "please cover my work."
